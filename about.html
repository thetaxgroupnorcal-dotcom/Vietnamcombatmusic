Vietnam Combat Music is a simple, original independent record label with a bigger purpose: carry forward the resilience of Vietnam veterans while helping artists turn their strongest work into sustainable careers, scalable platforms and creative legacies.

Who we are

Artist-first. Strategy-smart.

We do not force artists into a formula. We identify what makes each artist undeniable, then build the right music, story, audience and platform around that strength.

Vietnam veterans are remembered as one of America’s toughest generations—not because war should be romanticized, but because they endured combat, sacrifice, rejection and the long fight to be heard after coming home. Their strength was not noise. It was survival, loyalty, adaptability and the refusal to disappear.

That is the character of our label. We are resilient when the industry is difficult, direct when a story is uncomfortable and fiercely protective of the people who create the work. We believe independent artists deserve a platform as tough as the road that formed them—one capable of taking a hit, adapting and continuing forward without surrendering its identity.

Vietnam Combat Music combines independent-label agility with practical marketing, release and media strategy. We work with recording artists and creators across film, television, streaming, documentary, reality television, digital series and emerging formats. We help creators clarify their identity, develop meaningful bodies of work, connect with the audiences most likely to care and build systems that can grow without erasing what made the work special.

Our flagship catalog began with SP-5 Nelden J. Herron’s firsthand Vietnam combat poetry and the musical vision of Jesse Lawson, with featured creative contributions including Sadida. It shows exactly what we believe: singular stories become powerful when the right collaborators, presentation and distribution meet them.

The creative roster also includes Sadida Johansen, whose background is rooted in R&B and gospel, and Boston-rooted pop, hip-hop and Latin writer/producer Jennina Johansen.

Our defining legacy project

Truth deserved more than silence.

Jesse Lawson and Jennina Johansen took on what people said could not be done: transform difficult subjects and painful memories into music that could be heard, documented and remembered without losing the truth. Jesse built the musical bridge. Jennina protected the vision and carried the project forward. One identity, two roles

Music is the front door.
History is the foundation.

Vietnam Combat Music is the public-facing independent record label: direct, creative, artist-centered and built for music audiences.

Vietnam Combat Music Arts & History Corporation is the formal organization that owns and operates the label and supports its wider work across arts, history, preservation and creative enterprise.

That structure keeps the brand clear while giving every project room to grow—from a single song or screen placement to an enduring cultural archive. We also license, lend and loan approved music for aligned productions, including documentaries, historical projects and war films. Every use is reviewed individually and governed by a written agreement defining rights, term, territory, media and credit.
