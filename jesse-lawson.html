The artist

Built for the stage.
Driven by the song.

Jesse Lawson knows what it means to make music connect at scale—and what it means to choose purpose over momentum.

From 2010 through 2013, Lawson served as rhythm guitarist and backing vocalist for Sleeping With Sirens during the band’s defining early rise. His tenure spans the breakout era of Let’s Cheers to This, the acoustic EP If You Were a Movie, This Would Be Your Soundtrack, and Feel—the album that entered the Billboard 200 at No. 3.

Lawson stepped away from nonstop touring in 2013 to prioritize family and pursue a more personal creative path. He continued writing, recording and releasing independent music, including the crowdfunded Chapter II project and the album Projections. His official artist channel identifies him as an RIAA-certified platinum and gold artist/songwriter.

That combination—arena-tested instinct, songwriter sensitivity and studio craft—made Lawson uniquely equipped for Vietnam Combat Music: a project where the music could never overpower the testimony, but had to give it shape, tension, atmosphere and reach.

The collaboration

Making memory audible.

“The music does not decorate Nelden’s words. It opens a door into them.”

Working from Nelden J. Herron’s combat poetry and lived experience, Lawson helped transform firsthand testimony into a full musical language—part spoken-word record, part historical document, part emotional transmission.

His musical contribution is heard throughout the Vietnam Combat Music catalog, including songs featuring Jesse Lawson and Sadida such as “New Day,” “50 Gunner,” “Two Clicks Off,” “The Flamethrower,” “Welcome Home,” “Vietnam Christmas” and “No Love.” The physical release credits “Music by Jesse Lawson.”
