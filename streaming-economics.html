Who pays what—and how

Platform reality, not payout mythology.

YouTube

Use your channel RPM

YouTube defines RPM as actual revenue earned per 1,000 views after revenue share. It can include ads, Premium, memberships, Super Chat and other sources. The platform guarantees no fixed amount.

Official RPM explanation ↗ Best source for your real rate: YouTube Studio → Analytics → Revenue.

Spotify

No fixed per-stream rate

Spotify states that royalties are calculated by streamshare, not a fixed payment for each play. Approximately two-thirds of music revenue is allocated to recording and publishing royalties before rights-holder agreements determine what reaches an artist.

Official royalties guide ↗ Best source for your real receipts: distributor royalty statements.

Apple Music

Historical benchmark: $0.01

Apple reported an average of $0.01 per play for individual paid plans in 2020, including label and publisher royalties. Apple notes that value varies by plan and country; this is not a current guarantee or artist net payment.

Official Apple explanation ↗ Best source for current money received: distributor statements.

The number that matters

Gross platform royalties are not artist take-home income.

  • Recording and publishing royalties are different revenue streams.
  • Distributors, labels, collaborators, publishers and collection societies may receive different shares.
  • Country, listener plan, advertising demand, content type and monetized-view percentage change outcomes.
  • YouTube Shorts and long-form videos use different revenue systems.
  • Artificial streaming can trigger withholding, penalties or removal. Sustainable audiences matter more than manufactured counts.
